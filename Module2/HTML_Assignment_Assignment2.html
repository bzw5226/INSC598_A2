Assignment 1: Website Critique

Overview

For this assignment, you will critique an existing website in terms of its effectiveness. The purpose of the assignment is to analyze various aspects of web design applied in a specific case.

Identifying a Site

You can choose any site you want, you might want to use a site that you are familiar with, so that you can answer questions for the site.

Aspects of Your Critique

READ ME
The overall emphasis of your critique/analysis is the effectiveness of the design: content, format, navigation, etc. In critiquing/analyzing the website, be sure to justify your assessment. More specifically, your critique should cover at least the following in paragraph format (don't list your answers in bullet format). You should comment on each domain (e.g., "Users," "Purposes") but, you do not need to answer each element listed as considerations under the domains. These are just to help you find things to critique.


To aide you in focusing on each of the elements, I have developed a Google Doc in which you can collect your thoughts (Links to an external site.). Remember - the end product will convert this information into paragraph format.



  1. Users
    • What users is the site intended for?
    • Who are these users?
    • Are there different groups of users for the site?
    • What kind of (different) needs do they have?
    • Are there any special circumstances under which certain users access the website?
    • How well does the site satisfy the users' needs?

  2. Purposes
    • What are the intended purposes of the website?
    • Does it aim to inform people about the company or organization, to advertise a product, to provide a means for online purchase, to recruit new clients/volunteers, to convince people of a particular cause?
    • Most websites are designed with more than one purpose in mind. Which of these purposes are primary or more important?
    • Which purposes are secondary and less important? How well does the site accomplish its intended purposes Is the content of the website informative and persuasive?
    • Is it adequate, accurate, credible? Does it have the kind of information users would need?

  3. Content Design
    • Consider the purposes, users, and the context for the website. Is the content selection appropriate?
    • Is there too much or too little content?
    • Is the information cognitively within reach for the audience?
    • Is the information well organized?
    • Does the website achieve its intended purposes?

  4. Visual Appeal 
    • Is the site visually appealing? Is it easy to read? Are the graphics meaningful and well presented?
    • Is there a good balance between text and graphics? Does it make good use of white space?
    • Colors– Are there too many colors used on the site? Do colors go well together? Does color use complement the content, or is it more a distraction? Is it visually appealing?
    • Graphics– Are the graphics meaningful? Is there a clear rationale behind the use of every graphic on the site? Do the graphics enhance the content instead of distracting the reader? Are graphics of good quality? Are they too big or too small?

  5. Formatting & Layout 
    • Layout– Are different elements placed at meaningful places on the screen? Is there a good, clear focal point on the page? Does the page look balanced? Is the page too wide, too narrow, unbalanced, not print friendly?
    • Typography– Is the text readable? Is the typeface effective? Is it too big, too small?
    • Visual Hierarchy– Is there a clear visual hierarchy on the page?
    • Headings– Are headings formatted effectively?
  6. Navigation
    • Is it easy to navigate the site?
    • Is the site well structured to provide easy navigation?
    • Are navigational devices easy to identify and locate?
    • Is there a good rationale for the navigational scheme?

  7. Technology
    • Is the technology used for building the site appropriate? Is it adequate?
    • Would the design have been more effective if it had used some other technology?

  8. Miscellaneous
    • Depending on what site you're critiquing, there might be special issues to consider.
